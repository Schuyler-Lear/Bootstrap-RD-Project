About

I love anime, but the often slower pacing and requirement of your full attention often made it hard for me to watch the stories I was invested in. Especially as someone with ADHD, being able to read manga from my phone has made it tremendously easier, not to mention faster! Whether it's waiting before class, sitting on the bus, or even just while something's in the microwave, it's never not a good time to pull out your phone and read a quick chapter. For those interested in making the switch, here's a few things to know:

  • All manga on this list can be found on the Shonen Jump app! For just $2 a month, you can get unlimited access to these and many more, as well as new chapters as soon as they're out!
  • Most of these manga fall in the action/adventure genres!
  • Some of them do contain more mature and darker themes, which will be noted and displayed.
  • These are my opinions! If you dn't eel the same way about a certain story, that's ok! There are hundreds to choose from, and chances are you'll find something for you.

One Piece

Wealth, fame, power. One Piece, by Eichiro Oda, is about a pirate, Monkey D. Luffy, and his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, as they traverse the Grand Line in search of the pirate king's treasure. Its 1000+ chapters are full of some of the most unique, funny, and moving characters I've ever read. It may seem like a long read, but I've yet to meet a reader that hasn't said it's not long enough.

Read it Here!

Jujutsu Kaisen

Yuji Itadori was a normal highschooler until one fateful night. Through a series of actions that he might've had a hand in, he has become the vessel for the King of Curses, Sukuna. Yuji is quickly immersed in the world of Jujutsu sorcerers, and the cursed spirits they hunt. But big plans are in store for Yuji, both from friend and foe, and the spirit residing inside him.

Read it Here!

Sakamoto Days

Japan's strongest assassin meets a girl, has a family, and retires from the world of killing to open a small convenience store. What could go wrong? Taro Sakamoto's legacy is known throughout the assassin world, and trouble starts when a bounty is placed on his head. He will do whatever he has to to protect his family, but there's one rule he can't break: his wife will divorce him if he kills anyone.

Read it Here!

Chainsaw Man

Denji is a 16 year old boy that has lived his entire life paying off his fathers debts. And there's one main job he's doing: hunting devils. Devils appear on the earth when a concept or thing is feared. Denji has a devil partner though: The Chainsaw Devil, or as Denji calls him, Pochita. When they finally break free from there debts, Denji realizes it's time to live for himself - and is swiftly taken in by the Public Safety Devil Hunters. Under the ever watchful eye of his boss, Denji finally gets to live a normal life... kind of.

Read it Here!

One Punch Man

Saitama was just an ordinary salary man until one day, when he crossed paths with a monster. After getting beaten badly, he devoted himself to train for a whole year! Now, he's TOO strong! Every monster or villain he finds he beats in one punch. And he's getting bored fast. He needs to find stronger foes fast, and what better way than joining the hero association? And what threats out there might be too strong, even for him?
